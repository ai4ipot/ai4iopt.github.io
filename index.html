AI4IPoT

AI for Image Processing Applications on Traffic: Advancements, Challenges, and Opportunities
Co-located with 2024 IEEE International Conference on Image Processing (ICIP 2024), 27th-30th October 2024, Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates.

Scope and Topics

This workshop is conceived as a cutting-edge academic forum focused on the intricate challenges and opportunities within intelligent traffic systems. It ambitiously extends its scope to cover not just the analysis of adverse weather conditions in video imagery but also spans across a broad spectrum of research areas, including multimodal sensor fusion, traffic behavior analysis, accident liability determination, multi-agent navigation, population density estimation, traffic flow forecasting, alongside addressing the complex needs of autonomous vehicle operations and traffic regulation improvements. The aim is to gather thought innovators from the domains of image processing, machine learning, artificial intelligence, and computer vision, leveraging the latest advancements in high-performance computing, multimedia analysis, and secure signal processing. Serving as a vibrant platform for sharing pioneering research findings and discussions on AI's role in surveillance, driver assistance, with a special focus on the integration and implications of large-scale model frameworks. The workshop encourages dialogue between distinguished academics and industry professionals to foster innovation and outline new directions for joint research. This initiative not only aims to fast-forward developments in autonomous driving but also advances sophisticated traffic management strategies, ensuring a thorough examination of these areas in a collaborative setting. The topics may include but not limit to:

  1. Driving Behavior Analysis Techniques
  2. Intelligent Analysis of Traffic Violations
  3. Traffic Accident Investigation and Identification Techniques
  4. Vehicular and Roadside Sensing Technologies
  5. Multimodal Sensor Input and Fusion
  6. Large-scale AI Models for Traffic Management
